Calendula (Calendula officinalis)

A healing herb known for its skin-soothing properties and vibrant flowers.

Scientific Information

Family: Asteraceae

Native Region: Mediterranean

Plant Type: Annual herb

Height: 30-60 cm

Flowering Season: Spring to fall

Traditional Significance

Calendula has been valued across cultures:

  • Ancient healing herb
  • Traditional skin remedy
  • Medicinal flower
  • Cultural significance
  • Natural dye source

Medicinal Properties

  • Anti-inflammatory action
  • Wound healing
  • Antimicrobial effects
  • Skin soothing
  • Antioxidant benefits
  • Immune support

Common Uses

  • Skin care products
  • Wound treatment
  • Herbal tea
  • Natural dye
  • Cosmetic ingredient
  • Traditional medicine

Cultivation Tips

  • Well-draining soil
  • Full to partial sun
  • Regular watering
  • Deadhead flowers
  • Container suitable
  • Frost tolerant

Safety Precautions

  • Patch test for skin use
  • Avoid during pregnancy
  • Monitor for allergies
  • Use in moderation
  • Consult healthcare provider
  • Store properly
