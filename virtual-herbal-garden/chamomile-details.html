Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla)

A gentle healing herb known for its calming properties and widely used in traditional medicine for its soothing effects.

Scientific Information

Family: Asteraceae

Native Region: Europe and Western Asia

Plant Type: Annual herb

Height: 20-30 cm

Flowering Season: Early summer to fall

Traditional Significance

Chamomile has been valued across cultures for centuries:

  • Ancient Egyptians dedicated it to the sun god Ra
  • Used in medieval European herbal medicine
  • Known as "Earth's Apple" in Spanish tradition
  • Symbol of relaxation and peace
  • Traditional remedy for sleep and anxiety

Medicinal Properties

  • Natural sedative effects
  • Anti-inflammatory properties
  • Digestive system support
  • Mild antispasmodic
  • Skin healing properties
  • Anxiety-reducing qualities

Common Uses

  • Herbal tea for relaxation
  • Sleep aid preparation
  • Digestive discomfort relief
  • Skin care applications
  • Stress management
  • Natural hair lightening

Cultivation Tips

  • Prefers well-draining soil
  • Needs full to partial sun
  • Regular watering needed
  • Space plants 20-30 cm apart
  • Deadhead spent flowers
  • Self-seeds readily

Safety Precautions

  • May cause allergic reactions
  • Avoid if allergic to daisies
  • Consult before surgery
  • Can interact with blood thinners
  • Use moderately during pregnancy
  • Consult healthcare provider
