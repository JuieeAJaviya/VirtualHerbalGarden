Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea)

A powerful immune-boosting herb known as "Purple Coneflower" with remarkable healing properties for cold and flu.

Scientific Information

Family: Asteraceae

Native Region: North America

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Height: 60-120 cm

Flowering Season: Mid-summer to fall

Traditional Significance

Echinacea has deep roots in traditional medicine:

  • Used by Native American tribes
  • Traditional remedy for infections
  • Known as "Snake Root"
  • Historical use in wound healing
  • Symbol of strength and resilience

Medicinal Properties

  • Immune system stimulator
  • Natural antibiotic properties
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Antiviral capabilities
  • Wound healing support
  • Blood purifying qualities

Common Uses

  • Cold and flu prevention
  • Immune system support
  • Wound healing
  • Respiratory infections
  • Skin conditions
  • Herbal supplements

Cultivation Tips

  • Needs well-draining soil
  • Prefers full sun
  • Drought tolerant
  • Space plants 45-60 cm apart
  • Divide every 3-4 years
  • Winter hardy

Safety Precautions

  • May trigger allergies
  • Avoid with autoimmune conditions
  • Not for long-term use
  • Can interact with medications
  • Use caution during pregnancy
  • Consult healthcare provider
