Giloy (Tinospora cordifolia)

A powerful immunomodulator known as "Amrita" or the nectar of immortality in Ayurveda, famous for its life-enhancing properties.

Scientific Information

Family: Menispermaceae

Native Region: Indian Subcontinent

Plant Type: Climbing shrub

Height: Up to 300 cm

Flowering Season: Summer

Traditional Significance

Giloy holds immense importance in Ayurveda:

  • Known as "Amrita" (immortality)
  • Sacred in Ayurvedic texts
  • Used in ancient remedies
  • Symbol of longevity
  • Revered immune booster

Medicinal Properties

  • Immune system booster
  • Anti-inflammatory action
  • Antioxidant properties
  • Fever reduction
  • Digestive support
  • Blood purification

Common Uses

  • Immune enhancement
  • Fever management
  • Digestive health
  • Diabetes support
  • Stress relief
  • General wellness

Cultivation Tips

  • Well-draining soil
  • Partial to full sun
  • Regular watering
  • Needs support to climb
  • Annual pruning
  • Protect from frost

Safety Precautions

  • Monitor blood sugar
  • Avoid before surgery
  • Use with caution in pregnancy
  • May affect blood pressure
  • Start with small doses
  • Consult healthcare provider
