Lemongrass (Cymbopogon citratus)

A fragrant medicinal grass known for its citrusy aroma and powerful therapeutic properties in traditional medicine.

Scientific Information

Family: Poaceae

Native Region: South Asia

Plant Type: Perennial grass

Height: 90-180 cm

Flowering Season: Rarely flowers

Traditional Significance

Lemongrass has been valued across cultures:

  • Used in Asian medicine
  • Traditional fever remedy
  • Ancient digestive aid
  • Culinary importance
  • Aromatherapy essential

Medicinal Properties

  • Anti-inflammatory action
  • Antimicrobial effects
  • Digestive support
  • Anxiety reduction
  • Pain relief properties
  • Antioxidant benefits

Common Uses

  • Herbal tea preparation
  • Essential oil production
  • Culinary seasoning
  • Natural insect repellent
  • Aromatherapy
  • Digestive aid

Cultivation Tips

  • Well-draining soil
  • Full sunlight needed
  • Regular watering
  • Space plants 60 cm apart
  • Divide clumps annually
  • Protect from frost

Safety Precautions

  • May lower blood sugar
  • Use moderately
  • Avoid during pregnancy
  • Can cause skin sensitivity
  • Monitor blood pressure
  • Consult healthcare provider
