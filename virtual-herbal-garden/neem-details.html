Neem (Azadirachta indica)

A versatile medicinal tree known as "Nature's Drugstore" with powerful antibacterial and healing properties.

Scientific Information

Family: Meliaceae

Native Region: Indian Subcontinent

Plant Type: Evergreen tree

Height: 15-20 meters

Flowering Season: March to May

Traditional Significance

Neem has been revered in Indian tradition for centuries:

  • Known as "Sarva Roga Nivarini" (curer of all ailments)
  • Used in traditional ceremonies and rituals
  • Mentioned in ancient Ayurvedic texts
  • Symbol of good health and protection
  • Used in traditional farming practices

Medicinal Properties

  • Strong antibacterial properties
  • Natural antifungal agent
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Blood purifying properties
  • Immune system booster
  • Natural pesticide

Common Uses

  • Skin care and treatment
  • Dental hygiene maintenance
  • Hair care and growth
  • Natural pest control
  • Blood sugar management
  • Wound healing

Cultivation Tips

  • Prefers well-draining soil
  • Needs full sunlight
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Space trees 8-10 meters apart
  • Regular pruning recommended
  • Can grow in various soil types

Safety Precautions

  • Avoid during pregnancy
  • May affect fertility
  • Can interact with medications
  • Not recommended for infants
  • Start with small doses
  • Consult healthcare provider
