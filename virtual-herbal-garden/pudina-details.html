Pudina (Mentha spicata)

A refreshing herb known for its cooling properties and digestive benefits.

Scientific Information

Family: Lamiaceae

Native Region: Europe and Asia

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Height: 30-100 cm

Flowering Season: Summer

Traditional Significance

Pudina has been valued across cultures:

  • Ancient medicinal herb
  • Traditional digestive aid
  • Ayurvedic cooling agent
  • Natural breath freshener
  • Cultural culinary herb

Medicinal Properties

  • Digestive aid
  • Cooling properties
  • Antimicrobial action
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Respiratory support
  • Antioxidant benefits

Common Uses

  • Digestive health
  • Culinary seasoning
  • Herbal tea
  • Natural breath freshener
  • Essential oil
  • Traditional remedies

Cultivation Tips

  • Moist, well-draining soil
  • Partial to full sun
  • Regular watering
  • Container gardening suitable
  • Regular pruning
  • Protect from extreme heat

Safety Precautions

  • Use in moderation
  • Monitor for allergies
  • Consult during pregnancy
  • Watch for interactions
  • Use fresh leaves carefully
  • Store properly
