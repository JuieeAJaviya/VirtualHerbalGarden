Stevia

Scientific Information

Scientific Name: Stevia rebaudiana

Common Names: Stevia, Sweet Leaf, Sugar Leaf

Family: Asteraceae

Origin: Native to Paraguay and Brazil, now cultivated worldwide

Traditional Significance

Stevia has been used for centuries by the Guarani people of South America as a natural sweetener and medicinal herb. They called it "ka'a he'Ãª" which means "sweet herb". It has gained global popularity as a natural alternative to artificial sweeteners.

Medicinal Properties

  • Natural sweetening properties (200-350 times sweeter than sugar)
  • Zero-calorie natural sweetener
  • Anti-diabetic properties
  • Blood pressure regulation
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Antioxidant properties
  • Dental health benefits

Common Uses

  • Natural sugar substitute for diabetics
  • Weight management
  • Blood sugar control
  • Dental cavity prevention
  • Food and beverage sweetening
  • Traditional medicine

Growing and Cultivation

  • Grows best in warm, humid climates
  • Requires well-draining soil
  • Prefers full sun to partial shade
  • Can be grown in containers
  • Harvest leaves before flowering for best sweetness

Safety and Precautions

  • Generally recognized as safe (GRAS) by FDA
  • May interact with certain medications
  • Consult healthcare provider if pregnant or nursing
  • Start with small amounts to test tolerance
