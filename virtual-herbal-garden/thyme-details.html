Thyme (Thymus vulgaris)

A versatile aromatic herb known for its powerful antimicrobial properties and rich culinary history.

Scientific Information

Family: Lamiaceae

Native Region: Mediterranean

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Height: 15-30 cm

Flowering Season: Early summer

Traditional Significance

Thyme has been cherished throughout history:

  • Used in ancient Egyptian embalming
  • Symbol of courage in medieval times
  • Sacred herb in Greek temples
  • Traditional European medicine
  • Known for purifying properties

Medicinal Properties

  • Strong antimicrobial action
  • Natural expectorant
  • Anti-inflammatory effects
  • Rich in antioxidants
  • Immune system support
  • Respiratory health aid

Common Uses

  • Respiratory infections
  • Natural cough remedy
  • Culinary seasoning
  • Essential oil production
  • Natural preservative
  • Aromatherapy

Cultivation Tips

  • Needs well-draining soil
  • Prefers full sun
  • Drought tolerant
  • Space plants 30-45 cm apart
  • Trim regularly
  • Winter hardy

Safety Precautions

  • May affect blood clotting
  • Avoid before surgery
  • Use moderately in pregnancy
  • Can cause allergic reactions
  • Start with small amounts
  • Consult healthcare provider
