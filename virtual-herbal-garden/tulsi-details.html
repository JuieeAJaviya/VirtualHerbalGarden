Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Scientific Information

Scientific Name: Ocimum tenuiflorum

Common Names: Holy Basil, Tulsi, TulasÄ«

Family: Lamiaceae (mint family)

Origin: Native to the Indian subcontinent, now cultivated widely in Southeast Asia and other tropical regions.

Religious Significance

Tulsi is revered in Hinduism as a manifestation of the goddess Tulsi (also known as Vrinda), the consort of Vishnu.

Hindu Practices

Tulsi plants are often planted in Hindu homes, especially in the courtyard, and its leaves are used in rituals and offerings to deities.

Medicinal Properties

Known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, Tulsi is considered one of the most sacred and beneficial plants in Ayurvedic medicine.
